A struggle is approaching, sooner or later—a struggle much like that of the 
19th century, where we workers fought for the common good, where we secured 
8-hour workdays and better wage conditions. And all of this is about to 
change.

Technology has changed the world; it has made our lives easier and allowed the middle class to prosper for a brief moment. However, that technology has increasingly been usurped by Big Tech giants who seek to impoverish us.

We have handed the reins to large corporations that crave nothing but power, money, and global influence. They use our data to enrich themselves and, in doing so, distract us from the things that truly matter.

We are witnessing a world where precariousness and the automation of labor are becoming more evident every day.

This time, the world will bring a different kind of poverty; it will be unlike that of the 19th century. The poverty of the 21st century will look like this: there will be food, there will be water, and a place to sleep—but there will be no rest, and time will become the most precious commodity.

If these giant companies achieve the long-awaited AGI, I dread to think what will happen to labor. It could lead to a world where corporations seize total power, returning us to a model of technological aristocracy and their vassals.

But it doesn't have to happen that way. History has taught us that the people, united, will never be defeated.

That is why the new struggle will not come from strikes in the streets; it will come digitally. Hacking, the disruption of information systems, and the media battle—that will be the new battlefield.

We must fight for change. It wasn't easy for the people of the 19th century, but they succeeded. Now it is our turn to fight for a better world and a better future. As José Mujica said:

"_Politics is the struggle for the happiness of everyone, but especially for those who have been left behind. Do not let them steal your hope, for hope is the engine of those who have nothing left but their strength to fight._"
